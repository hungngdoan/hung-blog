AI Engineering Roadmap

I'm a software engineer with a background in cloud data engineering (Java, Python, GCP stack), and I'm currently pursuing UT Austin's online MSAI program (Fall 2026 start, targeting May 2028 graduation).

This page exists for one reason: to hold myself accountable in public. If I fall behind, you'll know.

My goal is to transition from data/cloud engineering into a production-focused ML Engineering or AI Infrastructure role. Not ML research. Not Kaggle competitions. I want to build AI systems that run in production, at scale, for real users.

Here's the plan, broken into three phases.

Phase 1: Foundation

Now through Summer 2027. Theme: bridge the gap between data engineering and ML engineering.

At work, I'm pushing to get involved in ML-adjacent projects. My cloud and pipeline experience is not something I need to escape. It's the foundation I'm building on. The engineers who can take a model from notebook to production are the ones companies actually need.

Milestones I'm committing to:

  • Complete pre-program self-study: PyTorch fundamentals, linear algebra review (Deisenroth et al.), and UT's Deep Learning course materials
  • Build and deploy at least one production-grade ML project (not a notebook, not a tutorial clone): Dockerized, tested, API-served, monitored
  • Start writing publicly about ML engineering patterns, what I'm learning, and where data engineering meets ML infrastructure
  • Contribute to at least one open-source ML infrastructure project (MLflow, Ray, vLLM, or similar)

What success looks like: by the end of this phase, my GitHub and writing should make it obvious I build ML systems, not just train models.

Phase 2: Depth and Proof

Fall 2027 through May 2028. Theme: go deep in coursework and build undeniable proof of competence.

This is the heaviest stretch of the MSAI program. Deep Learning, NLP, ML foundations, and Ethics all land in this window. The goal isn't just to pass. It's to come out of each course with something deployable.

Milestones I'm committing to:

  • Maintain strong standing across all foundational courses
  • Build a capstone-quality project that demonstrates end-to-end ML system design (data ingestion, training pipeline, serving, monitoring, user-facing interface)
  • Have a portfolio of 2-3 deployed projects that show range: NLP, computer vision, or recommendation systems, each with production characteristics
  • Sharpen algorithms fundamentals — LeetCode, system design thinking, ML-specific patterns. Keeps the problem-solving sharp and complements the coursework

What success looks like: by graduation, I should be able to design and build ML systems end-to-end, not just train models in notebooks.

Phase 3: Apply and Grow

Summer 2028 through 2031. Theme: apply everything I've learned and grow into a senior-level AI/ML engineer.

With the MSAI complete and a strong portfolio in hand, this is where the learning compounds. The goal is to own the full ML lifecycle — from model development through deployment and production monitoring — wherever that takes me.

Milestones I'm committing to:

  • Own or significantly contribute to at least one production ML system end-to-end
  • Continue publishing technical writing and open-source contributions to build reputation in the ML engineering community
  • By 2031, be operating at a senior level where I'm designing ML systems, mentoring others, and influencing technical direction

What success looks like: by 2031, "ML Engineer" isn't just a title. It's what I demonstrably am, backed by systems in production and a body of public work.

How to Hold Me Accountable

I'll update this page quarterly with progress. If a milestone slips, I'll say why. No vague excuses, no silent edits. The whole point of posting this publicly is that I can't hide from it.

If you're on a similar path, feel free to reach out. If you're ahead of me, I'd love to learn from you.
